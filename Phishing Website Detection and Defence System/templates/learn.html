Understanding Phishing Attacks

What is Phishing?

Phishing is a type of cyber attack where criminals disguise themselves as trustworthy entities to steal sensitive information such as usernames, passwords, credit card details, or other personal data. These attacks often come through email, text messages, or fake websites that mimic legitimate organizations.

Common Types of Phishing Attacks

Email Phishing

Mass-distribution of fraudulent emails impersonating legitimate organizations.

Spear Phishing

Targeted attacks directed at specific individuals or companies.

Whaling

Phishing attempts targeting high-profile executives or stakeholders.

Smishing (SMS Phishing)

Phishing conducted via SMS or text messages on mobile devices.

Vishing (Voice Phishing)

Fraudulent phone calls trying to extract sensitive information.

Clone Phishing

Replicating legitimate emails with malicious attachments or links.

How to Identify Phishing Attempts

  • Check the URL

    Look at the address bar. Phishing sites often use URLs that look similar to legitimate ones but have slight variations or misspellings. Always verify that you're on the correct domain before entering credentials.

  • Look for HTTPS

    Legitimate websites, especially those that handle sensitive information, should use secure connections (HTTPS). Look for the padlock icon in your browser's address bar.

  • Be Wary of Urgent Messages

    Phishing attempts often create a false sense of urgency to pressure you into acting quickly without thinking. Be suspicious of messages that demand immediate action.

  • Look for Poor Grammar and Spelling

    Many phishing attempts contain spelling errors, grammatical mistakes, or awkward phrasing. Professional organizations typically have copy editors review their communications.

  • Suspicious Sender Email

    Check the sender's email address carefully. Phishers often use addresses that look similar to legitimate ones but with slight variations.

Best Practices for Protection

Use Multi-Factor Authentication

Enable MFA whenever possible. This provides an additional layer of security even if your password is compromised.

Keep Software Updated

Regularly update your operating system, browsers, and security software to protect against known vulnerabilities.

Use a Password Manager

Create strong, unique passwords for each site and store them securely using a password manager.

Verify Before Clicking

Hover over links to see their destination before clicking. If in doubt, manually type the URL into your browser.

Be Cautious with Personal Information

Legitimate organizations typically don't request sensitive information via email or text messages.

What to Do If You've Been Phished

  1. Change Your Passwords - Immediately change passwords for any accounts that may have been compromised.
  2. Contact Financial Institutions - If you've shared banking details, contact your bank immediately.
  3. Enable Additional Security - Add extra security like two-factor authentication to your accounts.
  4. Monitor Your Accounts - Keep a close eye on your accounts for any suspicious activities.
  5. Report the Phishing Attempt - Use our reporting tool to help protect others from similar attacks.
